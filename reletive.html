Notice how only the visuals moved—other elements still behave as if these boxes and the image were in their original positions.

When to Use relative

  • For fine-tuning position (nudges) of labels, tooltips, icons, etc.
  • When you want to shift elements without breaking the overall layout flow.

When Not to Use relative

  • For elements that must overlap precisely or be taken out of flow—use absolute instead.
  • For headers or navbars that stay on screen during scroll—use fixed or sticky.
